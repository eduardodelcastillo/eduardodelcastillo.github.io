What It Costs You

Fifth Capital charges a management fee of 0.85% to 1.50%, depending on your balance, and is prorated across the entire year and is charged at the end of each calendar quarter (every three months). In other words, every three months Fifth Capital charges 0.2125% to 0.375% based on your average balance for the period. If you withdraw all of your money before the end of the quarter you are charged a prorated fee only for the days your money was managed by Fifth Capital.

Fifth Capital’s single management fee known as a “Wrap Fee” program covers everything you need including commissions, trades, transfers, rebalancing, advice, account administration, etc. Unlike other companies Fifth Capital does not charge you transaction fees to buy and sell securities.

Your fees are based on your average balance of your entire Fifth Capital account, not the gain or loss.

*The annual fee normally comes out of the return. For example, if your portfolio gained 10%, the net gain would be 8.50% based on a 1.50% annual fee.

How To Fund Your Account

We currently offer three funding methods, which must originate from a US-based financial institution.

1. Bank Transfer (ACH): This option lets you move funds directly between a checking or savings account and your Fifth Capital account. Once you confirm your bank account by identifying two very small deposits, we transfer your money electronically and allow you to make additional deposits at any time. In most cases, bank transfers will be deposited into your Fifth Capital account within two to four business days.

Once you have linked a bank account to your Fifth Capital account, you will also be able to schedule recurring deposits repeating weekly, biweekly (1st & 15th), monthly, or quarterly.

ACH Deposits are generally received within 2 business days, depending on the time of day when we submit/confirm your request. Your funds will be available for investment following a 4 business day hold after receipt to allows the funds to clear.

2. Wire Transfer: Should you choose this option, you will need to initiate a wire transfer from your bank account to your Fifth Capital account. In most cases, the funds will be deposited in your account the next business day after the wire transfer is initiated. Your funds will be invested immediately thereafter. Please note that most banks charge a fee for this service.

Wire Deposits are generally available for investment the same day of receipt.

Example: If you were to wire funds to us on Monday, your funds will be available for investment the same day provided the wire is received before 3:00 p.m., CST.

3. Account Transfer (ACATS): You can transfer an existing brokerage account held at another firm by selecting “transfer” as the funding method during the Fifth Capital signup process. This choice is optimal when closing or partially transferring assets from your old broker/dealer, because it avoids having to go in person to make the request. Please be sure that the title on your new account at Fifth Capital matches the account title at your current brokerage firm. You will also need to liquidate any mutual funds, futures or commodities that are part of your current portfolio, before initiating the transfer process. We cannot transfer mutual funds, futures or commodities.

Deposits originating from foreign banks or brokerages will be returned.

Deposits by check (401(k) Rollovers and IRA rollovers) are held for 7 business days to allow funds to clear your bank before being released for investment the following day.

Example: If your check is deposited on a Monday, your funds would be available for investment the following Wednesday.

They were rated ‘Best Online Broker’ for a third year in a row by Barron’s 2014.

Fifth Capital chose Interactive Brokers (IB) as its brokerage partner because:

  • 1. IB is the ONLY custodian that enables Fifth Capital to offer its clients a complete online experience. Other custodians would be inconvenient for Fifth Capital clients by requiring wet signatures on documents or funding via paper checks.

  • 2. IB’s singular focus on agency execution and clearing means that they are much lower risk than other custodians. IB does not engage in activities like proprietary trading which could put clients at risk. IB is solidly profitable and maintains regulatory capital that is above the regulatory requirement.

Rollover Your 401(k) into an IRA

To roll over a 401(k) into an IRA, open an IRA account on our website and select the “rollover” option during the application process. Once your IRA account is open, you should contact your 401(k) provider and instruct them to send a check for the entire value of your 401(k). The check should be made payable to Interactive Brokers LLC and should reference your account name and your IRA account number.

transfer your existing IRA to Fifth Capital

To transfer your existing IRA to Fifth Capital, just open an IRA account on our website and select the option to transfer an existing account. Complete the information and your IRA will automatically transfer in 5-10 business days. Please note that we will sell any positions in your existing IRA account upon transfer to Fifth Capital and reinvest the proceeds across our four core strategies.

consolidate a number of old 401(k) Rollover accounts into one

You are welcome to consolidate multiple 401(k)’s from previous employers into a single IRA account at Fifth Capital. We wish we could handle the 401(k) rollover process just like we can a normal brokerage account transfer, but 401(k) rollovers are a bit different. Your former employer has an exclusive relationship with your 401(k) administrator so the administrator will need to hear from you directly. If you have an IRA account open and funded with us already, you can use the rollover steps below to consolidate your 401(k) into your existing IRA account. If you do not have an IRA account open with us yet, during the account opening signup flow you can select "Rollover" and then "Traditional IRA" for the account type. For the rollover process itself, the vast majority of 401(k) providers will liquidate your account and send a check per your instructions.

Please note that some 401(k)’s contain both a traditional (pre-tax) component and Roth (post-tax) portion. If this is the case, you will need to open both a traditional IRA and a Roth IRA using our website by clicking the "open a new account" link on your Fifth Capital dashboard once you have one of the accounts open. The traditional component of your 401(k) should be rolled over to your traditional IRA account and the Roth component of your 401(k) should be rolled over to your Roth IRA account. In both cases your account must meet our $50,000 minimum.

When you call or email the 401(k) administrator to request a rollover, they will ask you how you want the rollover check made payable and where you want it sent. Please ask them to make the check payable to our brokerage partner "Interactive Brokers LLC," and reference your name and brokerage account number on the check. You can find your brokerage account number by logging in to your Fifth Capital account, on the “lower left side” of the page your brokerage account number should look something like “123xxxxx.” Please have them send the check to:

Interactive Brokers, LLC
8 Greenwich Office Park
Greenwich, CT 06831-0504
Attn: Cashier’s Department – IRA

*If you are opening a new IRA account with us for your 401K rollover, our website will provide the above rollover instructions as well.
